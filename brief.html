Section 01

What is ArgusX

ArgusX is a community-driven threat intelligence feed for the blue team infosec community. It is named after Argus Panoptes — the hundred-eyed giant of Greek mythology who never sleeps — paired with Ladon, the serpent guardian, which is the companion static file analysis tool built by the same author.

The platform has one core mission: connect Intel to Defense. When an analyst finds a malicious file, a new CVE being exploited in the wild, or a phishing campaign targeting their sector — they post it. When another analyst has a Sigma rule, a YARA detection, or a patch that stops the threat — they link their defense directly to the original Intel post. The relationship is visible, searchable, and exportable.

Tagline: Intel in. Defense out. — The platform is not just a feed of threats. It is a feed of threats paired with their solutions. Every Intel post has a "Defenses" section. Every Defense post links back to the threat it addresses. The graph view shows the full network of relationships.

Section 02

The Problem it Solves

The security community shares threat intelligence on X/Twitter, Mastodon, Slack, and Discord. This works — but badly. The signal-to-noise ratio is poor, findings are not structured, and there is no mechanism to connect a threat to its mitigation. An analyst who finds a malicious PDF and an analyst who has a Sigma rule that catches it may never connect.

Existing platforms like MalwareBazaar, VirusTotal, and OTX are excellent sources of raw data but are built for technical analysts. A non-technical employee at a defense contractor who receives a suspicious PDF has no place to quickly understand what it means or what to do.

ArgusX fills two gaps simultaneously:

  • For analysts — structured Intel and Defense posts with MITRE tagging, TLP classification, IOC export, and a relationship graph showing how threats connect to their mitigations.
  • For non-technical users — every post has a mandatory "Plain English" section explaining what the threat means and what would happen if exploited, written for someone who is not a security professional.
Section 03

Core Features

Two post categories

CategoryPost TypesPurpose
IntelIOC, TTP, CVE, DetectionA threat, vulnerability, or malicious indicator found in the wild
DefenseDefense, Detection Rule, Tool, PatchA Sigma rule, YARA rule, patch, mitigation, or tool that addresses a threat

Intel → Defense linking

When submitting a Defense post, the analyst selects the Intel post it responds to from a dropdown. This creates a bidirectional relationship: the Intel post shows available defenses, the Defense post shows what threat it addresses, and both appear connected in the Relationship Graph.

Relationship Graph

A canvas-based interactive graph showing threats and defenses as rectangular nodes connected by edges. Color-coded by type (red = Intel, green = Defense, amber = CVE). Filterable by campaign, threat actor, or post type. Clickable — single click shows summary, double-click opens the full post. Available both as a top-level feed view and embedded in every detail page.

Detail page

Every post opens a full detail page with three tabs: Overview (plain English summary, impact, technical findings, linked Intel and Defense cards), Relationship Graph (embedded, scoped to this post's connections), and Discussion (threaded forum scoped to this post).

Threaded discussion / forum

Each post has its own discussion thread. Rules are enforced: replies must be on-topic, minimum length enforced, IOCs must be defanged. Verified analysts are visually distinguished. Moderators can remove off-topic replies.

Share and export

Every post has a Share button with four options: copy direct link, share to Slack, export defanged IOCs to clipboard, download plain-text report.

TLP classification

Every post is classified at submission: TLP:WHITE (public), TLP:GREEN (community only), TLP:AMBER (limited sharing), TLP:RED (recipient only). Feed filters by TLP level. TLP:RED posts are visible only to the submitting user and designated recipients.

Live threat feed integration

ArgusX pulls from free public threat intelligence APIs (MalwareBazaar, ThreatFox, URLhaus, CISA KEV) to automatically populate the feed with current threats. Community members can then annotate, corroborate, or submit defenses against auto-ingested posts.

User Stories

Security Analyst
I received a suspicious PDF at work. I want to check if anyone in the community has seen it before, understand what it does in plain English, and find out if there is already a detection rule I can deploy.
Blue Team Lead
I want to post a Sigma rule I developed for a Gamaredon dropper and link it to the Intel post documenting the threat, so other defenders can find it and the relationship is captured in the graph.
CMMC Consultant
I need to show a defense contractor client what threats are currently targeting their sector (defense supply chain) and what mitigations are available, without requiring them to understand MITRE ATT&CK or read raw IOC feeds.

I am new to threat intel and want to understand what a polyglot PDF is, why it is dangerous, and what I can do about it — in plain English, not just a hash and an offset number.

I want to export a plain-text report of a threat and its available defenses to attach to a ticket or share with leadership who do not have ArgusX access.
Section 05

Data Model

Six core tables. All managed by Supabase (PostgreSQL).

Core schema
-- Users (populated from GitHub OAuth)
users
  id            uuid PRIMARY KEY
  github_handle text UNIQUE NOT NULL
  verified      boolean DEFAULT false
  created_at    timestamp

-- Posts (Intel and Defense)
posts
  id            uuid PRIMARY KEY
  author_id     uuid REFERENCES users
  category      text CHECK (category IN ('intel', 'defense'))
  type          text  -- IOC, TTP, CVE, Detection, Defense, Tool
  tlp           text CHECK (tlp IN ('WHITE', 'GREEN', 'AMBER', 'RED'))
  title         text NOT NULL
  body          text NOT NULL
  plain_english text NOT NULL
  impact        text
  ioc_code      text  -- hash, IP, domain, rule
  source_url    text NOT NULL
  mitre_tags    text[]
  campaign_tags text[]
  source        text  -- MalwareBazaar, Honeypot, etc.
  auto_ingested boolean DEFAULT false
  created_at    timestamp

-- Intel ↔ Defense relationships
post_links
  id            uuid PRIMARY KEY
  intel_post_id uuid REFERENCES posts
  defense_post_id uuid REFERENCES posts
  link_type     text CHECK (link_type IN ('responds_to', 'related'))

-- Upvotes (one per user per post)
upvotes
  user_id  uuid REFERENCES users
  post_id  uuid REFERENCES posts
  PRIMARY KEY (user_id, post_id)

-- Discussion comments
comments
  id         uuid PRIMARY KEY
  post_id    uuid REFERENCES posts
  author_id  uuid REFERENCES users
  body       text NOT NULL
  ioc_code   text
  created_at timestamp

-- Moderation flags
flags
  id         uuid PRIMARY KEY
  post_id    uuid REFERENCES posts
  flagged_by uuid REFERENCES users
  reason     text
  resolved   boolean DEFAULT false

Request flow

  1. User visits argusx.dev — static HTML served from GitHub Pages
  2. On load, Supabase JS SDK fetches posts from the database via REST API
  3. GitHub OAuth handles login — no custom auth server needed
  4. Post submissions write directly to Supabase via the SDK (row-level security enforces that users can only write their own posts)
  5. A Python cron job on Railway runs every 15 minutes, calls external feed APIs, and inserts new findings as auto-ingested posts with auto_ingested = true
  6. Row-level security in Supabase ensures TLP:RED posts are only readable by their author

No custom backend required for core functionality. Supabase handles the database, authentication, and REST API. The only custom backend code is the feed ingestion script, which is a simple Python file that calls external APIs and inserts rows. This is the same Railway + Python pattern already used by Ladon.

Example — MalwareBazaar API call (Python)
import requests, json

# Get recent malware samples tagged as polyglot or PDF
response = requests.post(
    'https://mb-api.abuse.ch/api/v1/',
    data={
        'query': 'get_taginfo',
        'tag':   'Polyglot',
        'limit': 10
    }
)
samples = response.json()['data']

# Insert each as an auto-ingested post in Supabase
for sample in samples:
    supabase.table('posts').insert({
        'category':      'intel',
        'type':          'IOC',
        'tlp':           'WHITE',
        'title':         f"MalwareBazaar: {sample['file_name']}",
        'body':          sample['signature'] or 'Auto-ingested from MalwareBazaar',
        'plain_english': 'This file was automatically flagged as malicious by MalwareBazaar. Community annotation welcome.',
        'ioc_code':      f"SHA256: {sample['sha256_hash']}\nFile type: {sample['file_type']}",
        'source_url':    f"https://bazaar.abuse.ch/sample/{sample['sha256_hash']}",
        'campaign_tags': sample['tags'] or [],
        'auto_ingested': True
    }).execute()
Section 09

Legitimacy & Moderation

The value of the platform depends entirely on the quality of its intel. Three layers prevent noise and false positives.

Layer 1 — Identity

GitHub OAuth is required to post. Every post is permanently attached to a GitHub handle. This eliminates anonymous submissions and creates accountability — a false positive under your real GitHub identity has reputational cost.

Layer 2 — Required fields

Every post requires a source URL. No source = no submission. This prevents speculation without evidence and gives readers a path to verify the claim independently.

Layer 3 — Community verification

  • Upvotes — community signals confidence in a finding
  • Dispute flag — any member can flag a post as disputed, triggering moderator review
  • Verified analyst badge — manually granted by platform admins to analysts with demonstrated track record. Verified posts are visually distinct and prioritized in search.
  • Discussion rules — enforced at UI level (minimum reply length, on-topic requirement) and by moderators. Off-topic = removal. Repeated violations = ban.
  • Auto-ingested posts — clearly labeled as coming from an external feed, not a human analyst. Community is invited to annotate and add context.

IOC handling: All IOCs displayed in the UI are defanged automatically — dots replaced, brackets added, @ signs encoded. The "Copy IOC" button re-fangs before copying to clipboard. This prevents accidental clicks on live malicious URLs in the feed.
